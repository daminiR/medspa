## WORKFLOW #1: 24-48 Hour Post-Treatment Complication Check-Ins

### Why This Wins

**Universal Applicability**: Works for ALL treatments - injectables, laser, skincare, chemical peels
**ROI**: Catch complications early = avoid ER visits, lawsuits, bad reviews
**Simplicity**: Simple SMS automation, no complex logic

### The Problem

Patients experience adverse reactions 24-48 hours post-treatment but don't know if symptoms are "normal" or dangerous. They either:
- Don't call (leads to worsening complications)
- Panic-call the office for normal symptoms (wastes staff time)
- Leave bad reviews because "no one checked on me"

### The Solution

```
Day 1 Post-Treatment (24 hours):
"Hi Sarah! Quick check-in after your [treatment].
How are you feeling? Reply with:
1 = All good
2 = Minor concerns (mild redness/swelling)
3 = Need to talk to someone

Your comfort is our priority!"

[If patient replies 3]:
AUTO-RESPONSE: "We'll call you within 30 minutes.
If urgent, call us now: [PHONE]"
[Alert staff member immediately]

Day 2 Post-Treatment (48 hours):
"Day 2 check! Most side effects peak now and improve
over the next week. Any concerns since yesterday?
Reply YES or NO."

[If YES]: Staff callback within 2 hours
```

### Key Features

- **Symptom-based triage**: 1-5 scale lets patients self-report
- **Immediate escalation**: "3" or keywords like "severe pain" trigger instant alerts
- **Covers all treatments**: Botox, fillers, lasers, peels, facials
- **Prevents lawsuits**: Documented proof you monitored patient safety

### ROI Metrics

- **Reduce complication severity**: Catch vascular occlusion within 24hrs vs. 72hrs = vision saved
- **Reduce staff time**: Automated check-ins vs. manual calls
- **Improve reviews**: "They actually cared and checked on me!" vs. "No one followed up"
- **Legal protection**: Timestamped records of proactive monitoring

### Implementation Effort

**Time**: 2-3 hours
**Requirements**:
- Add 2 message templates
- Add escalation logic (keyword detection)
- Train staff on response protocol

**No complex integrations needed**

## WORKFLOW #3: Pre-Treatment Prep Reminders (Reduce No-Shows & Complications)

### Why This Wins

**Universal Applicability**: Every treatment needs prep (stop retinoids, shave, no blood thinners)
**ROI**: Reduce no-shows, reduce complications, reduce wasted appointment time

Patients show up unprepared:
- Didn't stop retinoids ‚Üí skin too sensitive for peel ‚Üí reschedule
- Didn't shave for laser ‚Üí can't treat ‚Üí wasted slot
- Took aspirin day-of ‚Üí excessive bruising ‚Üí bad review
- Forgot to complete consent forms ‚Üí 20-minute delay

**Industry data**: 15-20% of appointments are rescheduled or delayed due to poor prep.

### The Solution

```

‚úÖ Avoid blood thinners (aspirin, ibuprofen)
‚úÖ No sun tanning or tanning beds

"3 days until your [treatment]! Prep checklist:
‚úÖ Still avoiding retinoids? (stopped 4 days ago)
‚úÖ No NSAIDs or blood thinners
‚úÖ Complete consent forms: [link]

‚úÖ Shave treatment area tonight (for laser)
‚úÖ No makeup tomorrow
‚úÖ Bring photo ID
‚úÖ Arrive 10 min early

```

### Key Features

- **Treatment-specific instructions**: Laser ‚â† injectable ‚â† peel prep
- **Progressive reminders**: Week, 3-day, day-before
- **Form completion nudges**: Link to consent forms with completion tracking
- **Reduce cancellations**: Patients feel prepared and confident

### ROI Metrics

### Implementation Effort

## WORKFLOW #5: "Peak Symptom Day" Educational Messaging (Reduce Anxiety Calls)

### Why This Wins

**Universal Applicability**: Chemical peels, laser, microneedling all have "worst day" during healing
**ROI**: Reduce panic calls to office, improve patient satisfaction, prevent bad reviews
**Simplicity**: Single educational message sent on predictable "worst day"

### The Problem

Patients experience the worst symptoms on predictable days (Day 3-4 of chemical peel = peak peeling, Day 1 post-laser = maximum swelling) and panic because they don't know it's normal.

**Result**:
- Flood of calls to office: "Is this normal?!"
- Staff time wasted reassuring patients
- Negative reviews: "No one warned me about this!"
- Some patients assume treatment "went wrong" and don't return

**Industry data**: 40-60% of post-treatment calls are for normal expected symptoms on peak days.

### The Solution

```
Chemical Peel - Day 3 Post-Treatment:
"The peel has begun! Days 3-4 are typically the
WORST for flaking. Here's how to handle it:

‚úÖ DO: Keep skin moisturized constantly
‚úÖ DO: Let it peel naturally
‚ùå DON'T: Pick, pull, or scrub
‚ùå DON'T: Use exfoliants

Makeup OK today if needed. Flaking = working!

This is NORMAL and means your treatment is
working. By day 7, new glowing skin emerges.

Questions? Reply anytime!"

Laser Hair Removal - Day 4 Post-Treatment:
"Day 4 Check-In: Hair Shedding Phase!

What's normal right now:
‚úì Hairs falling out on their own (this is the goal!)
‚úì Mild stubble as dead hairs push out
‚úì Slight peppering appearance

What to do:
‚Üí Let hairs fall naturally
‚Üí Gentle exfoliation with washcloth
‚Üí Continue SPF 30+ daily

‚ö†Ô∏è DO NOT tweeze or pick at hairs

This means your laser treatment is WORKING!
Any concerns? Reply or call [phone]"

Microneedling - Day 2 Post-Treatment:
"Day 2: Peak redness and swelling day!

NORMAL symptoms today:
‚úì Face looks red (like sunburn)
‚úì Mild swelling (especially around eyes)
‚úì Skin feels tight

```

### Key Features

- **Predictive timing**: Send on the exact day symptoms peak
- **Normalize symptoms**: "This is NORMAL" reduces anxiety
- **Clear dos/don'ts**: Actionable guidance, not just reassurance
- **Open communication**: "Reply anytime" makes patients feel supported

### ROI Metrics

**Reduce Staff Time**:
- 40-60% reduction in "is this normal?" calls

- "They checked in exactly when I needed it!" testimonials

**Prevent Bad Reviews**:
- "No one warned me" complaints drop to near-zero
- Patients prepared for worst-case = not shocked or upset

### Implementation Effort

**Time**: 2-3 hours
**Requirements**:
- Identify "peak symptom day" for each treatment type
- Create 1 educational message per treatment (3-5 total)
- Automated send on Day X post-treatment

**Extremely simple, high-impact**
