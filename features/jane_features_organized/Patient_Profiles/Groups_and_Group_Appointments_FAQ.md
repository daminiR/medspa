Getting Started with Groups and Group Appointments
‚≠ê What is a Group Appointment?
A Group Appointment involves multiple clients who do know each other and are attending the session or receiving the service together. These clients are billed under a single invoice that covers the session. You can decide which client will be billed for the appointment. An example of a Group Appointment is a couples counselling session.

take a look at our handy guide.

‚≠ê What‚Äôs the difference between Classes and Group Appointments?
Great question! There are a few differences between these session types:
Who are they for?
Classes
are for multiple unrelated clients, like a yoga or Pilates class.
Group Appointments
are for multiple (often related) clients joining a session together, like a Family Counselling session.
How are they scheduled?
Class
times must be scheduled administratively before clients can book online.
Group Appointment
times, however, can be chosen by clients online, just like 1:1 sessions.
Administrative scheduling
Classes and Group Appointments can be booked the same way as 1:1 visits
. Click anywhere on the schedule and then choose the Class or Group Appointment from the drop-down menu in the appointment panel.
Invoicing and taking payment
Class
participants are invoiced separately and they pay separately.
Group Appointments
have one
Primary Client
who is invoiced for the session. The invoice includes the names of
Secondary Clients
who have been arrived.
At this time, there is no option to split the bill between clients who attend a
Group Appointment
(we‚Äôre working on it though ‚Äî stay tuned üòâ).

‚≠ê Should Group Therapy be a Class or a Group Appointment?
Contrary to what you might think, Group Therapy sessions should actually be created as a Class for two reasons:
Class participants can all be billed and invoiced separately, whereas Group Appointments can only have one payee per session.
Group Therapy sessions usually involve participants who are unrelated, and Group Appointments are intended for clients who know each other and/or are related (eg. Couples or Families).

‚≠ê What permissions are enabled when a client adds a family member when booking a Group Appointment, and why?
When a client adds a family member or related profile, all permissions are enabled towards that client except permission to use their credit cards on file.

‚≠ê Can additional clients book into a Group Appointment on the Online Booking Site if there is already a participant in the appointment?
No, additional clients can not book a Group Appointment on your Online Booking Site if there is already at least one participant in the appointment. This is due to the nature of Group Appointments as they are intended for related clients.
For example, if you had a Group Appointment set up for family counseling, you wouldn‚Äôt want a stranger to be able to book a Group Appointment where a family member or multiple family members have already been added as participants.
For this reason, when a Group Appointment is booked online by a client, if they have linked

However, once participants are selected and the appointment is booked, Jane will remove that Group Appointment slot from the Online Booking Site so unrelated clients are not able to add themselves to the booking.
If needed, you are able to administratively add additional participants to the appointment in the Appointment panel.

Getting Started with Groups and Group Appointments
‚≠ê What is a Group Appointment?
A Group Appointment involves multiple clients who do know each other and are attending the session or receiving the service together. These clients are billed under a single invoice that covers the session. You can decide which client will be billed for the appointment. An example of a Group Appointment is a couples counselling session.

‚≠ê What‚Äôs the difference between Classes and Group Appointments?
Great question! There are a few differences between these session types:
Who are they for?
Classes
are for multiple unrelated clients, like a yoga or Pilates class.
Group Appointments
are for multiple (often related) clients joining a session together, like a Family Counselling session.
How are they scheduled?
Class

Group Appointment
times, however, can be chosen by clients online, just like 1:1 sessions.
Administrative scheduling
Classes and Group Appointments can be booked the same way as 1:1 visits
. Click anywhere on the schedule and then choose the Class or Group Appointment from the drop-down menu in the appointment panel.
Invoicing and taking payment
Class
participants are invoiced separately and they pay separately.
Group Appointments
have one
Primary Client
who is invoiced for the session. The invoice includes the names of
Secondary Clients
who have been arrived.
At this time, there is no option to split the bill between clients who attend a

‚≠ê Should Group Therapy be a Class or a Group Appointment?
Contrary to what you might think, Group Therapy sessions should actually be created as a Class for two reasons:
Class participants can all be billed and invoiced separately, whereas Group Appointments can only have one payee per session.
Group Therapy sessions usually involve participants who are unrelated, and Group Appointments are intended for clients who know each other and/or are related (eg. Couples or Families).

‚≠ê What permissions are enabled when a client adds a family member when booking a Group Appointment, and why?
When a client adds a family member or related profile, all permissions are enabled towards that client except permission to use their credit cards on file.
For example, if I add my child as a family member, I will (by default):
Receive a copy of my child‚Äôs receipts, statements, and financial emails.
Receive a copy of my child‚Äôs email notifications and reminders.

The only permission that will not be enabled by default is for me to use my child‚Äôs saved credit cards.
These permissions can be edited administratively, and they‚Äôre enabled by default so that parents or other related profiles can easily manage these different areas for minors or folks who need their support.

‚≠ê Can additional clients book into a Group Appointment on the Online Booking Site if there is already a participant in the appointment?
No, additional clients can not book a Group Appointment on your Online Booking Site if there is already at least one participant in the appointment. This is due to the nature of Group Appointments as they are intended for related clients.
For example, if you had a Group Appointment set up for family counseling, you wouldn‚Äôt want a stranger to be able to book a Group Appointment where a family member or multiple family members have already been added as participants.
For this reason, when a Group Appointment is booked online by a client, if they have linked
Client Relationship
profiles on their account, they will be prompted to select what participants they would like to book the appointment for. This will allow your clients to book multiple participants into the Group Appointment.
However, once participants are selected and the appointment is booked, Jane will remove that Group Appointment slot from the Online Booking Site so unrelated clients are not able to add themselves to the booking.
If needed, you are able to administratively add additional participants to the appointment in the Appointment panel.
