Insight #1: Mango Mint Package Pricing Problem

Source: MedSpa owner (dinner conversation, January 2026)

Software Used: Mango Mint

Business Type: New medspa (~1 year old), specializes in lasers and customizable treatments

What She Said (Raw Notes)

"When I talk to people on the phone, I try to sell them customizable packages - like for lasers and things like that. But then on the Mango Mint website or the portal, they see it listed as a package or something, and sometimes those packages are just cheaper. So people are like 'Why don't I just go for those cheaper versions?' It's causing problems because I'm a good seller on the phone but then they undercut themselves by seeing the cheaper options."

Additional context: She mentioned sometimes packages are like $10,000 and spread out over time. The customization and payment flexibility seems to be an issue.

The Problem (Explained)

This is a pricing transparency conflict between phone sales and self-service booking:

1

Phone Sales (High-Touch)

Owner does a consultation call. Based on client's specific needs, she creates a custom package - maybe $1,200 for a tailored laser treatment series.

2

Client Checks Portal

Client logs into Mango Mint portal or website. They see pre-built packages listed publicly - same treatments but at $900.

3

Lost Sale / Conflict

Client asks "Why should I pay more?" or just books the cheaper public option. Owner undercuts her own consultative sale.

Why Mango Mint Fails Here (Research Findings)

Issue Quote from Reviews/Research
Awkward Package Workflow "Packages could be built out better. Currently you have to charge the package in one invoice and then charge the service used in another step."
Inflexible Pricing "Timing and pricing customizations increased by a percentage instead of a flat dollar amount. With our hourly pricing model, this made pricing adjustments a manual process."
Limited Customization "You adapt to Mangomint's workflows rather than configuring it to match your existing processes."
No Unit-Based Tracking From SWOT analysis: "Can't track Botox units in packages" - no injectable unit package support

Our Opportunity

Build a package system that supports consultative selling:

  • Private/Custom Packages: Create packages that aren't visible on public booking but can be assigned to specific clients
  • Consultative Selling Mode: Create custom packages on-the-fly during phone calls without conflicting with website pricing
  • Client-Specific Pricing: Different clients can have different package prices without it being publicly visible
  • Flexible Payment Plans: Spread large packages ($10,000+) over time without showing "cheaper" bundled alternatives
  • Price-Match Protection: If a custom package is sold, client shouldn't see cheaper alternatives in their portal

User Story

"As a medspa owner who does consultative phone sales, I need to create custom package pricing for specific clients without those clients seeing different (cheaper) package options on the public booking site, so that I don't undercut my own sales."
