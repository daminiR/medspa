Taking Cash Payments and Calculating Change

To accept cash payments, you can use the Cash payment button. If the cash provided by the client is greater than the total of the sale, you can use the change calculator.

To enable the change calculator, open the Settings app and select Payments & Checkout > Advanced Settings.

During checkout, select the Cash payment button.

Enter the cash provided by the client in the Amount received field. The change will be automatically calculated for you. Select Confirm.

The sale will be marked as Closed.

If you are using the Cash Drawer app, your cash drawer will be updated automatically.

### 5. How Do I Fix An Accidentally Voided Sale

**Source**: [https://www.mangomint.com/learn/how-do-i-fix-an-accidentally-voided-sale/](https://www.mangomint.com/learn/how-do-i-fix-an-accidentally-voided-sale/)

Fixing an Accidentally Voided Sale

If a sale is accidentally voided, you can add it back into your reports by creating a new sale.

To create a new sale that is identical to the voided sale, begin by opening the Sales app and selecting New Checkout.

If the sale was assigned to a specific client, make sure you select the same client.

If the sale included a service or product assigned to a specific staff member, make sure you assign the same staff member.

Link copied
New sale:

Make sure the total of the new sale matches the total of the voided sale, including any tips.

Close the new sale using the Other payment button. You can add a note about the voided sale for reference. Closing a sale using the Other payment button will not charge the clientâ€”it simply records the sale in Mangomint.

If the new sale was closed on a different date than the voided sale, change the date of the new sale to reflect when the original payment was collected. To do this, open the Sales app and select the new sale.

Select Change Date from the "..." menu and specify the date when the original payment was collected.

The sale is now updated, and your reports will reflect the correct information.

Client notes

Client notes (also called global notes) are comments or reminders added to a client's profile that are internal only and not visible to the client. Client notes are useful for things like client allergies or other important information about the client.

Client notes appear prominently when booking, viewing, or checking out an appointment for the client. These notesÂ are not connected to a specific appointment or date.

To add client notes, go to the client details and add the notes in the Notes section.

After adding client notes, the notes will appear when booking, viewing, or checking out an appointment for the client.

Link copied
Timeline notes

Timeline notes have a date, appear in chronological order on the client's timeline, and can have attached files such as photos or PDF documents. These notes can be used to keep track of color formulas, product recommendations, etc. Timeline notes are internal only and not visible to the client.

When adding a timeline note, you can use the current date, or select a different date. Once the timeline note is added, you can edit or print the note using the "..." menu on the note.

After saving your changes, an appointment icon displays to indicate the appointment has notes. This appointment icon also displays if a client adds a comment to their appointment via online booking.

Appointment notes will also appear on the client timeline.

Link copied
Sale notes

Sale notesÂ are comments or reminders added to a specific sale.

To add sale notes to a sale, open the Sales app and select the sale.

Resolving a Remaining Balance

When a refund is completed in a closed sale, the sale will have a status of Closed (Remaining Balance) if:

An amount was refunded instead of line items.

The sale had multiple payments, and only a single payment was refunded.

To ensure you have accurate reports, you will need to resolve the remaining balance. However, sales with a Closed (Remaining Balance) status are still closed and will still be included in Sales reports and the Payroll report.

Link copied
Resolving a remaining balance

To resolve a remaining balance in a closed sale, select the sale and specify how to resolve the balance.

Select refunded items: This option allows you to assign the refunded amount to specific line items in the sale, allowing you to also adjust commission, add products back to your inventory, and more.

Collect payment: This option allows you to collect a new payment for the refunded amount. This is helpful if the client requested a refund so they could use a different payment method.

Link copied
Select refunded items

If you chose Select refunded items, you can resolve the remaining balance by assigning the refunded amount to specific line items in the sale.

A separate refund will be created for the sale, which will keep track of how the balance was resolved and which line items were refunded.

The status of the sale will change to Closed (Partially Refunded) or Closed (Refunded), depending on whether the sale is partially or fully refunded.

Link copied
Collect payment

If you chose Collect payment, you can resolve the remaining balance by collecting a new payment for the refunded amount. This is helpful if the client requested a refund so they could use a different payment method.

Once the payment is complete and the balance is resolved, the status of the sale will change to Closed.

Using Mangomint with a Booth Renter or Hybrid Model

Mangomint can support businesses that have a booth rental or hybrid model.

Booth renters are service providers who are usually charged a flat or variable fee to rent space (booth, chair, room, etc.) within a salon, spa, or studio instead of getting paid an hourly rate or commission by the business.

A hybrid model refers to a business that has booth renters and other types of workers, including service providers who are paid hourly or commission.

Businesses that have a booth renter or hybrid model can use one Mangomint account to easily manage all their workers.

To learn more about managing booth renter and hybrid models, watch our on-demand Booth Renter & Hybrid Models video.

Link copied
Mangomint features for booth renter & hybrid models

Mangomint includes the following features that can be used with booth renter and hybrid models:

Payment (merchant) accounts: Booth renters can have their own payment (merchant) account, allowing them to receive payments directly to their own bank account.

Taking payments at the booth or on the go: Booth renters can process payments at their booth or on the go by connecting their own card reader to their iPhone or iPad.

Direct booking links: Clients can book directly with a booth renter by using the booth renterâ€™s direct booking link.

Client ownership: Booth renters can be assigned as the owner of a client. This ensures they will only have access to their own clients.

Product ownership: Booth renters can be assigned as the owner of a product. This ensures they will receive the funds when a product they own is sold.

Collecting rent payments from booth renters: You can collect rent payments from booth renters automatically using a membership.

Payment (merchant) accounts

Booth renters can access their assigned payment account to see how much they made. They will be paid according to the payout schedule. Booth renters are only responsible for per-transaction fees, which will be deducted from their payouts.

By default, all gift card, package, and membership sales go into the primary payment account for the business, even if the booth renter has their own payment account. Any gift cards, packages, or memberships that are redeemed will need to be paid out to the booth renter separately.

Collecting rent payments from booth renters

You can collect rent payments from booth renters using a membership. This can be helpful if you want to automatically collect rent payments from booth renters when rent is due. This method works best when booth renters are charged the same amount of rent each billing period.

To charge and collect rent via Mangomint using a membership, you will need to:

Create a booth rent service and set the service price to the rent amount.

Create a booth rent membership plan. Set the billing frequency to match how often you charge rent, disable the Charge service tax toggle, and add the booth rent service to the membership plan.

Create a client profile for each booth renter.

Start a new booth rent membership for each booth renter. You can specify whether to collect membership payments automatically or manually for each booth renter.

Membership payments are considered deferred revenue. They will appear in the Membership Payments report, but they will not appear in the Sales Summary report unless the membership services are redeemed.
