Staff Management Research Insights

Competitive analysis from Mangomint, Boulevard, Zenoti, Vagaro reviews + industry forums | January 2026

KEY FINDING

"People don't need more features, they just need more speed."

Should We Build Staff Self-Service Portal?

Verdict: Probably Not (as a separate thing)

Nobody explicitly asks for "self-service portal" - zero search results. It's table stakes, not a differentiator.

What Staff Actually Want

Want Evidence
See their schedule on phone "Staff want to see their schedule 24/7" - multiple sources
Know their commission/pay Payroll transparency reduces HR questions
Request time off easily Current: scattered across systems, texts, emails

What Staff DON'T Want

  • Fill out their own onboarding forms (admin does this faster)
  • Update their own profile info (they just want to work)
  • Learn another app/portal

Future Idea: Simple Staff Mobile App

Read-only view of: My Schedule | My Commission | My Upcoming Shifts

Not for onboarding or data entry - just visibility. Could be PWA, not native.

Commission & Payroll Problems HIGH PRIORITY

Biggest mess across all platforms

Problem Evidence
Tiered commissions confuse everyone $27K sales ≠ 10% of $27K - involves 3 tiers of calculation
Settling tips takes forever "One by one" for larger teams - Mangomint
No tip prompt for memberships When package total is $0, provider must awkwardly ask
Cash vs card tips confusing Different payout timing, different tracking
Fee-splitting is legally risky Medical staff can't get volume-based commission
Manual spreadsheets persist 15% fully manual, 31% use software AND spreadsheets
Payroll errors cost $1,600/employee/year Industry average
Half quit after 2 payroll mistakes Research finding

What Makes Staff Quit

  • Schedule ignores their requests - placed on shifts they didn't want
  • No control over schedule - 39% say manageable schedule would reduce burnout
  • Unfair hours distribution - newer employees sometimes get more hours
  • Payroll mistakes - nearly half leave after two mistakes
  • No training/development - 94% would stay if company invested in learning
